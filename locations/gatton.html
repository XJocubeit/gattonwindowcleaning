Why Gatton Windows Are Different

LOCAL CONDITIONS THAT
MAKE PROFESSIONAL CLEANING MATTER

Gatton's environment is uniquely demanding on windows. The combination of agricultural dust, seasonal pollen loads, bore water mineral deposits, and Queensland's UV intensity means standard DIY cleaning rarely achieves a lasting result, and the wrong technique can actually accelerate glass degradation over time.

Red Dust & Dry Spells

The Lockyer Valley's red soil goes airborne during dry periods and settles across every exposed surface. It's slightly abrasive, when left to bond through alternating rain-and-dry cycles, it can permanently etch and dull glass. Regular professional cleaning every 3–4 months prevents irreversible damage and keeps your view crystal clear year-round.

Heavy Agricultural Pollen

Flowering crops and farmland surrounding Gatton produce some of the highest pollen loads in South East Queensland. Pollen packs densely into track grooves and sill corners, staining frames yellow and blocking drainage channels if left uncleaned. Left long enough, organic staining can permanently discolour UPVC frames and require professional treatment to reverse.

Bore Water Mineral Scale

Many Gatton and surrounding Lockyer Valley properties use bore or tank water for irrigation. Sprinkler overspray onto glass leaves white calcium and silica deposits that won't come off with standard cleaning products, they require acid-based or diamond-pad treatment. Xavier identifies and treats mineral scale as part of any affected clean, at no extra cost for standard cases.
