Why Hatton Vale Windows Are Different

LOCAL CONDITIONS THAT
MAKE PROFESSIONAL CLEANING MATTER

Hatton Vale's mix of new estates and agricultural surroundings creates a unique window-cleaning challenge. Construction dust, highway traffic film, and rural field drift combine to dirty glass faster here than in most parts of the valley.

Construction Dust & Silica

Ongoing residential development across Hatton Vale means fine silica dust from earthworks and building sites is almost always in the air. This particulate bonds to glass surfaces during wet-dry cycles and can permanently etch new glass if not removed regularly with proper professional technique and chemistry.

Highway & Traffic Film

Properties near the Warrego Highway corridor pick up a fine diesel exhaust and rubber particulate film that ordinary cleaning can't shift. This traffic film is oily in nature, it smears when wiped with a cloth and actually traps other dust on top of it. Professional degreasing solution and squeegee technique are the only effective approach.

Agricultural Field Drift

The farmland bordering Hatton Vale's residential areas generates persistent airborne dust and pollen drift, particularly during dry spells and harvest periods. Combined with the Lockyer Valley's prevailing westerly winds, this agricultural contamination reaches even the newest estates and deposits a gritty film that accumulates rapidly on glass and in track channels.
