Regency Downs sits at the agricultural heart of the central Lockyer Valley. Its combination of suburban density, farmland proximity, and summer storm exposure creates window-soiling conditions that are more demanding than they appear from the street.

Surrounding farmland generates heavy spring and summer pollen loads that drift into Regency Downs on prevailing westerly winds. Combined with fine red Lockyer Valley soil dust from cultivation and bare paddocks, glass here accumulates a distinctly gritty film that requires proper degreasing chemistry, not water alone, to remove without smearing.

Storm Residue & Summer Grime

Regency Downs is in the path of the summer thunderstorm corridor that builds off the Main Range. Track Compaction & Mould

In older established Regency Downs homes, window tracks often haven't been properly cleared in years. Xavier clears all track material as step two of every clean, before any moisture touches the glass, preventing this cycle from continuing.
