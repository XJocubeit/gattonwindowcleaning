About This Service

WHAT SCREEN
CLEANING INVOLVES

Screens are the first line of defence against insects and dust, but that means they also collect everything first. Dust, pollen, spiderwebs, fly remains, and airborne debris all build up in the mesh over time. A dirty screen doesn't just look bad; it restricts airflow and can actually push grime back onto your freshly cleaned glass.

Xavier removes every screen from its frame, takes it to a clean work area, and washes it thoroughly with a purpose-made soft brush and cleaning solution. Each screen is fully rinsed, dried, and carefully reinstalled so it sits flush and functions properly.

The process covers all screen types, standard flyscreens, security mesh screens, aluminium frames, and retractable fly screens, with technique adjusted to match the material.

How We Clean Your Screens

OUR 4-STEP
SCREEN PROCESS

Every screen. No shortcuts.

1

REMOVE & INSPECT

Every screen is removed from its frame carefully. Before we clean anything, we inspect for existing damage, bent frames, tears in the mesh, broken tabs. You'll know upfront if anything needs attention.

No surprises. No hidden damage.

2

WASH & SCRUB

Taken to a clean work area, each screen gets scrubbed thoroughly with a purpose-made soft brush and cleaning solution. We work the mesh from both sides, dislodging dust, pollen, spider webs, and fly remains that have built up over time.

Soft brush. Purpose-made solution. Mesh-safe.

3

RINSE & DRY

After scrubbing, every screen is fully rinsed to remove all cleaning solution and loosened debris. Then dried properly before reinstallation, a wet screen going back into the frame carries water into the track and defeats the whole purpose.

Fully rinsed. Dried before reinstall.

4

REINSTALL & CHECK

Each screen is reinstalled carefully in the correct window, flush fit, tabs seated, no gaps around the edges. We test that it opens and closes properly. If a screen needs a second look, it gets one before we call the job done.

Flush fit. Operation tested. Job done right.
